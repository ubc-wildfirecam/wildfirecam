About the Project

Protecting BC Through Innovation

The UBC WildfireCam Network is a collaborative wildfire monitoring research initiative led by the University of British Columbia Okanagan (UBCO) in partnership with the BC Wildfire Service (BCWS).

The network blends advanced camera technology, real-time data analysis, and machine learning algorithms to support wildfire monitoring, improve situational awareness, and enhance emergency preparedness across the province.

Integrated with BC Wildfire Service Operations, the provincial WildfireCam system ensures that all collaborating partners have access to the information they need to support decision-making and inter-agency cooperation.

Key Benefits

Why WildfireCam Matters

The provincial camera network will be the first integrated and publicly accessible wildfire monitoring network in Canada.

Enhanced Situational Awareness

Real-time visual intelligence for emergency responders and the public — empowering faster, more informed decisions when every second counts.

Consistent Detection & Monitoring

Continuous, real-time visual data capture day and night. Our cameras never sleep, providing unwavering vigilance across BC's landscapes.

Better Decision Making

Well-informed and coordinated strategic response. Camera data enables wildfire services to deploy resources precisely where and when they're needed.

"With the support of our partners, we aim to deploy cameras across the province to support wildfire preparedness, response, and monitoring — creating a new standard for community safety in Canada."

Dr. Mathieu Bourbonnais Lead Researcher, Fire Ecology and Remote Sensing Lab — UBC Okanagan

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about how WildfireCam works, its capabilities, and how it protects BC communities.

WildfireCam is a research project out of the Fire Ecology and Remote Sensing Lab led by Dr. Mathieu Bourbonnais at the University of British Columbia's Okanagan campus. Data, including images and videos, from cameras will be used to develop smoke-detection algorithms for wildfire detection, inform wildfire modelling in Canadian ecosystems, and inform research in post-fire landscape recovery.

Analysis of potential camera sites are conducted, and optimal viewsheds are selected to monitor landscapes around critical infrastructure, culturally significant sites, and other at-risk areas. The optimal sites are locations which have tall infrastructure, access to power, and connectivity to ensure the best sightlines for the cameras and uninterrupted video/image uploads.

At this time, only project supporters including the BC Wildfire Service, and fire departments in areas with cameras will have access to camera feeds through private login access.

Increased awareness means more informed decision making. While every fire may not require response, cameras allow provincial wildfire services to monitor and deploy resources as needed. Additionally, cameras which pick up fire starts show teams exactly where a fire is located. This means that wildfire responders don't need to 'chase smoke' to determine where the fire is, how large it is, and how best to respond.

Privacy and data governance for the wildfire camera initiative are maintained in strict accordance with UBC Policy SC14 and Information Security Standards, ensuring full compliance with BC's FIPPA regulations. To maintain domestic oversight, all data is hosted exclusively within UBC Datacenters, precluding the routing of information through foreign servers. Furthermore, sensitive data is masked via edge-processing at the camera source. Access is managed through a Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) framework, limiting visibility to authorized personnel for the primary purpose of smoke and fire detection in remote regions.
